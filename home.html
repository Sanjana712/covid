• COVID-19:

    • COVID-19 is the disease caused by a new coronavirus called SARS-CoV-2. WHO first learned of this new virus on 31 December 2019, following a report of a cluster of cases of viral pneumonia in Wuhan of China. Among those who develop symptoms, most (about 80%) recover from the disease without needing hospital treatment. About 15% become seriously ill and require oxygen and 5% become critically ill and need intensive care. Complications leading to death may include respiratory failure, Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS), sepsis and septic shock, thromboembolism, and/or multiorgan failure, including injury of the heart, liver or kidneys.<

  • SYMPTOMS:

    • Most common symptoms:
    1. Fevere
    2. Dry cough
    3. Triedness
    Less common symptoms:
    1. Aches and Pains
    2. Sore Throat
    3. Diarrhoea
    4. Headech

  • PREVENTION:

    • Clean your hands often. Use soap and water.
    • Maintain a safe distance from anyone who is coughing or sneezing.
    • Wear a mask when physical distancing is not possible.
    • Don't touch your eyes, nose or mouth.
    • Cover your nose and mouth with your bent elbow or a tissue when you cough or sneeze.
    • Santize your surroundings.
